## Public Skate vs Freestyle Skate

**Public skate** is general skating time for everyone. It is the right fit for date nights, families, and first-time skaters. The goal is simple: skate safely, have fun, and build confidence.

**Freestyle** is practice-oriented ice time, typically used by figure skaters and advanced skaters working on specific skills. Freestyle sessions usually have stricter etiquette so skaters can train without collisions.

If you are not sure, start with public skate. Once you can control speed and stop confidently, freestyle becomes a great next step.

## Tips for Your First Time Ice Skating in Nashville

* Wear warm layers and comfortable socks that are not too thin and not overly thick.
* Choose a quieter session if possible, since crowded ice can feel intimidating.
* Start near the boards, then gradually move to open ice as you stabilize.
* Learn two skills first: gliding and stopping. Everything improves after that.
* Expect a small fall or two. Try to fall to the side and move to the boards before standing.

## Nashville Ice Skating FAQ

### Do I need my own skates?

No. Most public sessions offer skate rentals. Owning skates helps with comfort and consistency if you skate regularly.

### Is public skate okay for beginners?

Yes. Public skate is the best starting point. Just go slowly, stay aware, and use the boards when needed.

### What should I wear ice skating?

Dress for cold air and movement. Wear layers and pants you can bend in. Gloves are a good idea for beginners.

### What time should I arrive?

Plan to arrive 20 to 30 minutes early so you have time for parking, check-in, and rentals.

### What is freestyle ice time?

Freestyle is practice-focused ice time, often used by figure skaters. It typically has different etiquette than public skate.

### Are there lessons for kids and adults?

Yes. Lessons are commonly available for both kids and adults. Start on your preferred rink page to find the official program links.
