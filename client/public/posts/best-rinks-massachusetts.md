# Best Ice Skating Rinks in Massachusetts: From Boston to the Suburbs

Massachusetts has a rich and layered ice skating tradition. The state gave us the Skating Club of Boston — one of the most prestigious figure skating clubs in American history — and dozens of community rinks from the South Shore to MetroWest where generations of families have learned to skate. Whether you're a competitive figure skater, a hockey player, or a first-timer, here are the best ice skating rinks in the Bay State.

---

## The Elite Tier

### Skating Club of Boston — Brighton

The [Skating Club of Boston](/rink/skating-club-of-boston-norwood-ma) is in a class by itself. Founded in 1912 and relocated to a modern three-sheet facility in Brighton on Soldiers Field Road, the SCB has produced more national and Olympic champions than perhaps any other skating club in the United States. Names like Tenley Albright, Peggy Fleming, and countless others trained here. The club offers public skating sessions as well as a world-class freestyle and figure skating program. Adult admission is around $15, with skate rental available. Coaches must be affiliated with the club to coach on its ice.

If you're serious about figure skating — or just want to experience one of the sport's most historic venues — this is the place.

### New England Sports Center — Marlborough

For sheer scale, nothing in New England matches [New England Sports Center in Marlborough](/rink/new-england-sports-center-marlborough-ma). With eight full-sized sheets of ice, it's one of the largest ice facilities on the entire East Coast. NESC hosts major figure skating competitions, dozens of hockey tournaments annually, and has daily public skating and freestyle sessions. Adult admission is around $13. If you can't find ice time here, you can't find it anywhere.

## Boston City Rinks

### Steriti Memorial Rink — North End

Operated by the City of Boston, [Steriti Memorial Rink](/rink/steriti-memorial-rink-boston-ma) sits in the historic North End neighborhood along the waterfront. It's a full-size indoor rink open year-round with some of the most affordable admission in Greater Boston — just $6 for adults with $4 skate rentals. The rink supports youth hockey and figure skating programs and is beloved by the surrounding neighborhood community.

### Bajko Memorial Rink — Hyde Park

[Bajko Memorial Rink in Hyde Park](/rink/bajko-memorial-rink-boston-ma) is another City of Boston facility, this one on the south side near the Dedham line. Affordable city skating at $6 for adults with year-round programming for all ages.

### Brighton Arena — Brighton

[Brighton Arena](/rink/brighton-arena-brighton-ma) is a City of Boston facility in the Allston-Brighton neighborhood, one of Boston's most densely populated and community-oriented areas. Full-size indoor sheet, $6 admission, $4 rentals. Popular with local hockey leagues and families from the Allston/Brighton/Brookline corridor.

## South Shore Rinks

### Hobomock Arena — Pembroke

A South Shore favorite, [Hobomock Arena in Pembroke](/rink/hobomock-arena-pembroke-ma) is a two-sheet facility serving Plymouth County and surrounding communities. Adult admission is around $10 with active figure skating and hockey programming. Convenient for skaters in Pembroke, Hanson, Whitman, and surrounding towns.

### Canton Sportsplex — Canton

[Canton Sportsplex](/rink/canton-sportsplex-canton-ma) is a two-sheet facility in Canton serving the southern suburbs including Stoughton, Sharon, Norwood, and Walpole. Adult admission is around $10, and the rink runs a solid Learn to Skate program. The pro shop offers sharpening throughout the day.

---

## MetroWest / Central MA

### Valley Sports Arena — Concord

[Valley Sports Arena in Concord](/rink/valley-sports-arena-concord-ma) serves the MetroWest and Route 2 corridor with two full sheets and year-round programming. Adult admission is around $11. Located in historic Concord near the Acton and Maynard lines, it's well-positioned for skaters from a wide area of Middlesex County.

---

## Tips for Skating in Massachusetts

**Boston's city rinks are exceptional value.** At just $6 for adults, the city's public rinks — Steriti, Bajko, Brighton — offer quality skating at some of the lowest prices in the state. For budget-conscious families, they're hard to beat.

**The SCB is worth experiencing.** Even if you're not a competitive skater, attending a public session at the Skating Club of Boston is a special experience given its history and the caliber of skating you'll see.

**MetroWest has excellent options.** The Route 9 and Route 20 corridor is dotted with quality facilities. New England Sports Center in Marlborough is the obvious anchor, but Valley Sports Arena in Concord and Canton Sportsplex are also worth knowing.

**Winter is busy — plan ahead.** Massachusetts rinks fill up fast from October through March. Weekend public sessions at popular facilities should be booked online in advance.
