# Best Ice Skating Rinks in Illinois: Chicago and Beyond

Illinois offers a rich ice skating landscape — from the iconic outdoor experience of skating for free beneath the Chicago skyline at Millennium Park to serious training at multi-sheet suburban arenas that host regional and national competitions. The Chicago area alone has more quality ice rinks than most states, and the options extend well into the suburbs and beyond. Here are the best ice skating rinks in Illinois.

---

## Downtown Chicago

### Millennium Park Ice Rink — Chicago

There's no more dramatic skating backdrop in the Midwest than [Millennium Park Ice Rink](/rink/millennium-park-ice-rink-chicago-il). Set beneath the Pritzker Pavilion bandshell and adjacent to the iconic Cloud Gate (known as "The Bean"), this outdoor rink is open from late November through early March and admission is completely free — you only pay if you rent skates ($14). With a 16,000-square-foot surface and a magical urban setting, it's one of the truly great free skating experiences in the country. This isn't the place for serious training, but for a memorable public skating experience, it's unbeatable.

---

## Chicago Park District — Indoor Rinks

### McFetridge Sports Center — Chicago

When you want year-round indoor skating in the city, [McFetridge Sports Center](/rink/mcfetridge-sports-center-chicago-il) is the go-to. Operated by the Chicago Park District, McFetridge features two full NHL-sized sheets and runs active figure skating and hockey programs throughout the year. Adult admission is around $8 — excellent value for city skating. The facility is located in the Irving Park neighborhood on the Northwest Side.

---

## Chicago Suburbs — DuPage County

### Seven Bridges Ice Arena — Woodridge

For scale and quality, [Seven Bridges Ice Arena in Woodridge](/rink/seven-bridges-ice-arena-woodridge-il) is one of the finest suburban facilities in the Chicago area. With three full NHL-sized sheets, the complex hosts major figure skating competitions, runs an extensive freestyle program, and supports both youth and adult hockey leagues. The pro shop offers professional sharpening with custom hollow selection. Adult public skating is around $12. If you're looking for the most comprehensive suburban ice experience in the Chicago area, Seven Bridges is it.

### Fox Valley Ice Arena — Geneva

[Fox Valley Ice Arena](/rink/fox-valley-ice-arena-aurora-il), operated by the Fox Valley Park District, serves the western suburbs including Aurora, Geneva, Batavia, and St. Charles with two full sheets and year-round programming. Park District pricing makes it very accessible at around $9 for adults.

---

## Northwest Suburbs

### Twin Ice Arena — Elk Grove Village

Village-operated with two full sheets, [Twin Ice Arena in Elk Grove Village](/rink/twin-ice-arena-elk-grove-village-il) serves the northwest suburbs with quality programming at affordable rates — around $8 for adults. Convenient for skaters in Elk Grove Village, Schaumburg, Hoffman Estates, and neighboring communities.

### Northbrook Sports Center — Northbrook

A well-respected Northbrook Park District facility, [Northbrook Sports Center](/rink/northbrook-sports-center-northbrook-il) has a strong figure skating tradition and two full sheets. Adult admission is around $9, and the rink offers daily freestyle sessions and a solid Learn to Skate program. Serves the North Shore suburbs including Deerfield, Glenview, and Wheeling.

---

## South Suburbs

### Homewood-Flossmoor Ice Arena — Flossmoor

[Homewood-Flossmoor Ice Arena](/rink/homewood-flossmoor-ice-arena-flossmoor-il) is a two-sheet facility serving the south suburbs — Flossmoor, Homewood, Olympia Fields, and surrounding communities — with year-round programming. Adult admission is around $10.

### Iceland Ice Arena — Oak Lawn

[Iceland Ice Arena in Oak Lawn](/rink/iceland-ice-arena-oak-lawn-il) serves the southwest Chicago suburbs with two full sheets and active figure skating and hockey programs. Adult admission is around $10, and the rink has an established freestyle program. Located on 95th Street, it's convenient for skaters throughout southwestern Cook County.

---

## Tips for Skating in Illinois

**Millennium Park is free — take advantage of it.** The free outdoor admission at Millennium Park is genuinely exceptional. The only cost is skate rental ($14). It's best experienced on a weekday or during a snowfall.

**Suburban rinks offer great value.** Park district-operated facilities in the Chicago suburbs typically charge $7-$10 for adults and maintain quality ice. They're an excellent deal compared to many urban markets.

**Seven Bridges is the competition hub.** If you're a figure skater looking for a large facility that hosts formal competitions, Seven Bridges in Woodridge is the premier suburban venue in the Chicago area.

**Hockey leagues are everywhere.** Greater Chicago has a massive adult hockey community. Most suburban rinks have multiple levels of adult leagues, from beginner to A-level competitive.
