# Ice Skating in Nashville: Your Complete Guide to Every Rink

Nashville is not the first city that comes to mind when you think about ice skating. But Music City has more ice than most people realize — a year-round indoor rink right in the heart of the city, three more run by the Nashville Predators organization, and a growing skating community that includes everything from learn-to-skate programs for toddlers to competitive figure skating clubs.

This guide covers every option for public ice skating in the Nashville area, what each rink is best for, and how to decide which one to visit.

## Centennial Sportsplex

For the complete guide, see our [Centennial Sportsplex ice skating guide](/blog/centennial-sportsplex-ice-skating).

Centennial Sportsplex is Nashville's original public skating destination and still the most affordable option in the area. Located in the West End neighborhood adjacent to Centennial Park, it is operated by Metro Nashville Parks and Recreation and has been open since 1990.

**What makes it unique:** Two full-size ice sheets (200 x 85 feet each), making it the only multi-rink public facility in Nashville proper. The west arena serves as the Nashville Predators practice facility, meaning the ice is maintained at a professional standard. Admission is $6-7 and now includes skate rental — one of the best values in Tennessee for public skating.

**Best for:** Families on a budget, beginners, learn-to-skate programs, anyone who wants to walk in without a reservation.

**The catch:** Public skate sessions run on a variable schedule rather than fixed weekly hours. Always check the Centennial Sportsplex Ice Arena Google Calendar on the Nashville.gov website before making the trip. Sessions are typically offered Wednesday, Friday, and Saturday but this changes based on hockey league bookings and special events.

**Address:** 222 25th Ave N, Nashville, TN 37203
**Phone:** 615-862-8480

## Ford Ice Center — Antioch

For the complete guide, see our [Ford Ice Center Antioch guide](/blog/ford-ice-center-antioch).

Ford Ice Center Antioch is the southernmost of the three Nashville Predators-operated public rinks. Located in the Antioch area off Murfreesboro Road, it is the closest option for residents of south Nashville, Brentwood, and Murfreesboro.

**What makes it unique:** Ford Ice facilities are cashless and require online reservations in advance — a different experience from the walk-in model at Centennial. The $12 admission includes skate rental. The facilities are newer, cleaner, and more modern than Centennial, with a polished experience that reflects the Predators organization's investment in skating infrastructure across Middle Tennessee.

**Best for:** Skaters who plan ahead, south Nashville and suburban residents, hockey players who want a modern facility.

**The catch:** No walk-ins. You must reserve your spot online before arriving. This eliminates the spontaneous "let us go skating tonight" option but also means you are never surprised by a full rink.

## Ford Ice Center — Bellevue

For the complete guide, see our [Ford Ice Center Bellevue guide](/blog/ford-ice-center-bellevue).

Ford Ice Center Bellevue serves the west Nashville and Bellevue corridor, making it the most convenient option for residents of west Nashville, Brentwood, and the western suburbs.

Same format as Antioch — cashless, online reservation required, $12 admission with rental included. The Bellevue location is the most recently built of the Ford Ice facilities and tends to be the quietest of the three, which makes it a good option if you want more open ice during public sessions.

**Best for:** West Nashville and suburban residents, skaters who want a less crowded session, families comfortable with online booking.

## Ford Ice Center — Clarksville

For the complete guide, see our [Ford Ice Center Clarksville guide](/blog/ford-ice-center-clarksville).

Ford Ice Center Clarksville extends the Predators skating network north of Nashville into Clarksville, Tennessee — about 45 minutes from downtown. For residents in the Clarksville, Springfield, or north Davidson County areas, this is the closest public ice option.

Same pricing and reservation model as the other Ford Ice locations. The Clarksville facility also serves as a hub for youth hockey development in the Montgomery County area.

**Best for:** North Nashville and Clarksville area residents, youth hockey families in the northern suburbs.

## Which Nashville Rink Should You Choose?

A few simple rules to help you decide:

**Go to Centennial Sportsplex if:**
- You are a first-timer and want to keep it low-key and affordable
- You have young kids and want to walk in without planning ahead
- You are enrolling in a learn-to-skate program
- Budget matters — at $6-7 it is roughly half the price of Ford Ice

**Go to a Ford Ice Center if:**
- You want a guaranteed spot and are comfortable reserving online
- You prefer a newer, more modern facility
- You are closer to Antioch, Bellevue, or Clarksville than to West End
- You or your kids are interested in hockey programming

## Learn to Skate in Nashville

Both Centennial Sportsplex and the Ford Ice Centers offer structured learn-to-skate programming following the Learn to Skate USA curriculum.

At Centennial, the Nashville Skating Academy runs group learn-to-skate sessions for all ages, grouped by skill level. Six-week sessions include instruction plus supervised practice time, with skate rental included. It is one of the most affordable structured skating programs in Middle Tennessee.

Ford Ice Centers run their own learn-to-skate programming through the Predators organization, with group lessons available at all three locations.

If you or your child is serious about learning — not just trying skating once — enrolling in a structured session is worth it. The difference between learning in a lesson versus figuring it out during public skate is dramatic. Progress happens much faster with instruction.

## Ice Skating Near Nashville: Other Options

**Gaylord Opryland Resort** offers seasonal outdoor ice skating during the holiday season as part of their So Much Christmas programming. It is a fun experience but very different from a standard rink — smaller ice surface, holiday atmosphere, higher pricing. Worth doing once as an experience, not a training ground.

**Murfreesboro:** Ice skating searches from the Murfreesboro area appear frequently in Nashville-area queries. The closest dedicated ice rink for Murfreesboro residents is Ford Ice Center Antioch, about 20-25 minutes north. There is currently no public ice rink in Murfreesboro proper.

**Franklin and Brentwood:** No dedicated public rink currently. Ford Ice Bellevue and Centennial Sportsplex are both reasonable drives from most Franklin and Brentwood addresses.

## Planning Your Visit

A few universal tips that apply to any Nashville rink:

**Dress in layers.** All indoor rinks run around 55-60 degrees on the ice surface. Bring a light jacket regardless of the season outside. Summer visitors consistently underestimate this.

**Wear the right socks.** One pair of thin, calf-height socks gives you the best skate fit. Thick socks or two pairs reduce ankle support inside the boot.

**Bring gloves.** When you fall — and you will — your hands hit the ice first. Even thin athletic gloves make a significant difference.

**Arrive early.** Whether you are walking in at Centennial or have a reservation at Ford Ice, arriving 15-20 minutes ahead of your session gives you time to get skates, lace up, and not feel rushed stepping onto the ice.
